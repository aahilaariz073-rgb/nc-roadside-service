How Much Does Semi Truck Tire Replacement Cost in 2026?

NC Roadside Service LLC - Charlotte Metro - 24/7

It's the question every owner-operator asks the second a tire goes: what's this going to cost me? The honest answer is "it depends" -- on the size, the position, whether you go new or used, and whether you're paying for someone to bring it to you. This guide breaks down the real ranges so you walk in knowing roughly what to expect, instead of getting a number out of thin air.

A quick note up front: tire pricing moves with rubber and freight costs, and every situation is different. Treat the numbers below as ballpark ranges, not quotes. For an exact price, call us with your size and position and we'll give you a straight number.

The short answer

For a single commercial truck tire in 2026, most owner-operators are looking at roughly:

  • New steer or drive tire: generally a few hundred dollars per tire, varying by brand and size.
  • Quality used tire: often a good bit less -- a popular way to keep a tight budget moving.
  • Mounting and service: added on top, and higher if it's mobile or after-hours.

That spread is wide on purpose, because a budget drive tire and a premium long-haul steer aren't in the same world. Let's narrow it down.

What drives the price

Four things move the number more than anything else:

  • Tire size. Common sizes like 11R22.5 and 295/75R22.5 are widely stocked and competitively priced. Specialty sizes and super singles (like 445/50R22.5) tend to run higher.
  • Position. Steer tires carry safety and handling demands, so drivers often spend more there and save on drive or trailer positions.
  • New vs. used. The single biggest lever on price. More on that below.
  • Brand tier. Premium long-haul brands cost more up front but can deliver more miles; value brands lower the up-front hit.

New vs. used: the real trade-off

This is where most of the cost decision lives.

New tires cost more up front but give you full tread life, a known history, and peace of mind for long hauls. If you run high miles or want maximum time before the next replacement, new usually pencils out.

Quality used tires cost noticeably less and can be the smart move when you're managing cash flow, need to get rolling now, or are replacing a position that doesn't justify a premium tire. The key word is quality -- a good used tire from a reputable source with solid remaining tread is very different from a worn-out casing. We carry both new and used, and we'll tell you honestly which makes sense for your route.

There's no universally "right" answer. A long-haul owner-operator and a local hauler replacing a trailer tire will reasonably land in different places.

If you call a mobile unit instead of limping to a shop, there's a service component on top of the tire. But it's worth weighing against what a tow plus shop time actually costs you.

How to get an accurate quote fast

You'll get a real number quickest if you have these ready when you call:

  • New or used preference -- or ask us to recommend based on your route.

With that, we can quote you straight over the phone -- no surprises when we show up.

Frequently asked questions

Is it cheaper to repair or replace a semi tire?
If the damage is a repairable tread puncture on a good casing, a repair is far cheaper than replacement. Sidewall damage, blowouts, and worn casings mean replacement is the safe call. We assess it on-site and tell you which applies.

Are used semi tires worth it?
Quality used tires with solid remaining tread can be a smart, budget-friendly choice -- especially for non-steer positions. The key is sourcing from a reputable provider rather than an unknown worn casing.

How much does mobile tire service cost vs. a shop?
Mobile adds a service component, but it usually beats a tow bill plus shop labor plus hours of downtime. For a working truck, the time saved often outweighs the difference.
